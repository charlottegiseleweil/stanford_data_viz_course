Evaluating the " 25 by 25 " policy

Through interactive data visualization   Data source: Bryant and Lempert, 2010

This visualization is inspired by examples from Dave Deriso's course at Stanford. The libraries jquery, bootstrap slider, D3, processing and crossfilter were used.

Exploring the (combination of) inputs leading to the cases where a policy fails to meet its performance goals.

The approach taken in this visualization aims to support the scenario discovery method (Thinking inside the box: A participatory, computer-assisted approach to scenario discovery). It aims to point out the scenarios where a policy fails to meet its performance goals, in order to highlight the vulnerabilies of considered policy.

Scenario discovery characterizes such sets by helping users to apply statistical or data- mining algorithms to databases of simulation-model-generated results in order to identify easy-to-interpret combinations of uncertain model input parameters that are highly predictive of these policy-relevant cases. The approach has already proved successful in several high impact policy studies. Bryant and Lempert, 2010

This visualization aims to lead to the same result, by allowing the user to select subsets of each input parameters, and viewing the result in policy cost (as number of cases leading to each cost range, cost ranges being the quantiles of total cost ditribution, see histogram below). The considered case involves a proposed U.S. renewable energy standard. 882 cases were generated (through a simulation creating a database to explore all combinations of the 9 uncertain input parameters)
